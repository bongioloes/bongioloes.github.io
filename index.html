Brazil nut productivity estimates
in secondary forest

This is my master thesis. I got to collect data in the Amazon rainforest and process it later. I develop the first growth and production model for Brazil nut Bertholletia excelsa in secondary forest. The research involved field data collection, data managing (excel and R), model construction (Python), statistical analysis (R), and elaboration of the final map of Brazil nut productivity (R). I also got to present the research to a variety of people, from research peers to a non-technical audience.

Governance and Infrastructure
In the Amazon

I was part of the Community of Practice and Learning Focused on Governance and Infrastructure in the Amazon (GIA). More specifically, I was part of the Geospatial group, where our objective was to tackle the project using remote sensing and GIS methods and data to analyze the results from a geographic point of view. My key role was the creation of the visualizations (paper maps) that were used in the participatory map exercise. Later on, I was also responsible for developing the result's visualization (digital map).
